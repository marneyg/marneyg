Natives of Rossville

Interviews

Jim Channel / Lois Lacock / Joyce Brennan / Duane Parr / Clarence Wehner

Jim Channel

Birthdate: 6/9/61

Years Lived in Rossville: 27

Year Graduated from RHS: 1979

Favorite Place in Rossville: My mom’s house at dinnertime

Occupation: Painter/Coach

1. What were some activities you were involved in as a child?
     Sports, basketball, baseball, football, track

2. What were some fun places to go?
     Friends’ houses and the C & W Café (present-day Dawg House) pinball machine

3. What was one of the biggest changes that have happened in Rossville?
     Water shed ponds-slowed flooding down on Cross Creek and a new high school was built

4. What was your first job?
     At my Dad’s grocery store-Channel’s Grocery  (present-day TruValue Hardware Store)

5. What was one of the things you miss the most from Rossville?
     Channel’s Grocery and the old high school
6. What was the most memorable event in Rossville?

     Floods and the Rossville Centennial ‘71 (10 years old)

Lois Lacock

Birthdate: 7/25/44

Years Lived in Rossville: 43

Year Graduated from RHS: 1962

Favorite Place in Rossville: My home

Occupation: Secretary at RGS

1. What were some activities you were involved in as a child?
     4-H, Cheerleading, Pep Club, and Sunday School

2. What were some fun places to go?
     Teen town, which was in the backroom of the restaurant, dances at the community center and shelter house, and the movie theater, which was upstairs at Wehner's Grocery

3. What are some of the biggest changes to take place in the city of Rossville?
     The population growth, destruction of the old high school, and a park-lined with trees

4. What was your first job?
     Babysitting and cleaning houses

5. What was one of the things you miss the most from Rossville?
     Trees at park

6. What was the most memorable event in Rossville?

     The day she got married at old Methodist Church.  Extra: During drivers Ed a person in her group backed straight into a tree in the park

Joyce Brennan

Birthdate: 4/27/29

Years lived in Rossville: 71

Year Graduated from RHS: 1947

Favorite place in Rossville: Home

Occupation: Retired

1. What were some activities you were involved in as a child?
     Pep club, band, and chorus-solos

2. What were some fun places to go?
     Movies (10 people per car), ball games, skating rinks, first dance permitted when junior in high school, and home parties

3. What was one of the biggest changes that have happened in Rossville?
     Grown in population, not farming community like it used to be, modernization (when I was a  junior in college my family got our first TV, and we got our first phone when in high school)

4. What was your first job?
     Rossville Truck and Tractor while in high school and Santa Fe Railroad

5. What was one of the things you miss the most from Rossville?
     My parents and all my high school friends that moved away

6. What was the most memorable event in Rossville?
     The Centennial celebration

Duane Parr

Birthdate: 10/2/21

Year lived in Rossville: 80

Year Graduated: 1939

Favorite place in Rossville: Home

Occupation: Farmer

1. What were some activities you were involved in as a child?
     School activities, I should have gone to the country school but I came to Rossville

2. What were some fun places to go?
     Dance Hall every Saturday night

3. What was one of the biggest changes that have happened on Rossville?
     The state moved Highway 24 to south of railroad tracks

4. What was your first Job?
     Worked on farms, cultivated corn for $1 a day

5. What was one of the things you miss the most from Rossville?
     Open ear movies on Main Street

6. What was the most memorable event in Rossville?
     A softball game when I got hit with a bat, 1934 (12 years old)

Extras:

Big Stockyard (present-day Swirly Top)

Model T delivering 300-pound blocks of ice to people in Rossville

International tractor pulled Model T's through Highway 24 when the road was out

7 to 8 filling stations in Rossville at one time

Tree nursery north of present-day Miller Motors 

A house moved with a team of horses to make room for Highway 24
