ROSSVILLE FACT BOOK

Rossville is located about 20 miles northwest of Topeka, the capital of Kansas.  The Oregon Trail ran through the middle of Rossville.  As a matter of fact, two separate trails, one from Papin's Landing (Topeka) and the other from Uniontown (Willard) merged together in Rossville ( Refer to the Oregon Trail ).  Cross Creek runs from north to south through the city.  The Kansas River is situated about three miles south of town.

OTHER INTERESTING FACTS:

Rossville's original name was Edna.  Why the change?  The United States granted the citizens of Edna a post office, but under the name Rossville (Refer to the Founding of Rossville).  Edna was primarily located on the west side of Cross Creek.  Colonel Veale and A. C. Sherman built a depot on the east side of the creek, which caused most people to abandon Edna and move to the new site of Rossville.

Rossville's Nickname in 1881 - "The Queen City of the Kansas Valley"
