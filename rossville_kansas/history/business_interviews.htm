2001 Business Owner Interviews

Doug Perine / Roger Braden / John Wehner

Doug Perine

Birth Date: 1/31/51

Years Lived in Rossville: 24

Year Graduated: 1969

Favorite Place in Rossville: My Pharmacy

Occupation: Pharmacist

Year Business Started: 5/23/77

Years in Rossville: 24

1. How did you start your business?
     Sold house in Manhattan for the rest of money to buy building

2. How has business changed throughout the years?
     Gotten bigger, 1980 tripled in size, 1984 bought KPL building to the North, 1987 bought Grace White’s house to the South, 1997 bought room behind Flower Mart

3. How have the prices differed from then until now?
     1977-$7.00
     2001-$45.00

4. How do you keep your customers interested?
     Advertise once a month, sell seasonal merchandise, and large amount of over the counter prescriptions

5. How does your business affect Rossville?
     Nursing home, Dr. Leinwetter, Local service

6. How has your business changed with the internet and the larger businesses in Topeka?
     Website www.healthmart.com order prescriptions over net shows prices, sizes, free delivery in Rossville, Topeka, seven small towns

Extras: Started buying prescriptions over internet instead of wholesale

Roger Braden

Birth Date: 8/9/1951

Years Lived in Rossville: 23

Year Graduated: 1969

Favorite Place in Rossville: My Home

Occupation: Sales

Year Business Started: 1953

Years in Rossville: 48

1. How did you start your business?
     It was started by Luther "Buck" McLain from scratch.  He first began delivering gas.

2. How has business changed throughout the years?
     The business first just delivered gas, then we added propane.  "Buck" bought the station on Highway 24 in 1964.  The parts store was added in 1980.  Today, we sell gas, parts, tires, and pizzas.

3. How have the prices differed from then until now?
     The price of gas when I started was about $.20 a gallon and as of today 4/25/01 it is a $1.62 a gallon.

4. How do you keep your customers interested?
     By having a good product at a fair price.  Many people from the local area like to buy from local businesses.

5. How does your business affect Rossville?
     It provides jobs for the younger people and pays sales tax to the city.

6. How has your business changed with the internet and the larger businesses in Topeka?
     Sometimes products are cheaper on the internet and we lose some business to Topeka because in some cases they can offer cheaper prices.

John Wehner

Birth Date: 10/21/1928

Years Lived in Rossville: 44

Year Graduated: 1946

Favorite Place in Rossville: True Value Store

Occupation: Merchant

Year Business Started: 1949

Years in Rossville: 44

1. How did you start your business?
     My grandfather George Wehner and my father John J. Wehner opened the first store.

2. How has business changed throughout the years?
     There is more self-service.

3. How have the prices differed from then until now?
     The price of bread for example was about $.10 and now it is a $1.69 or higher.

4. How do you keep your customers interested?
     We have good service and prices.

5. How does your business affect Rossville?
     We have people from other communities come into Rossville to buy products.

6. How has your business changed with the internet and the larger businesses in Topeka?
     Scanning groceries has been a big help.  We are opening a new store soon that will be three times larger than our present facility.  Our hardware store is on the internet at www.truevalue.com.

Extras: Our new store will be opening soon and as I said previously it will be three times larger than our present store.  We will have a deli and a larger variety of goods on hand.  I opened a rental store three years ago and it has done very well.
